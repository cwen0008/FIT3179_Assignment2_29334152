World Economies by GDP per capita in USD

The Gross domestic product (GDP) per capita is the monetary value of a country's total economic output in a year, divided by its population. It represents the average income of a country's residents before taxes.

Understanding a country's GDP per capita is an important initial step in gaining insights into the country's economic conditions and requirements, as well as the overall quality of life experienced by the average citizen. A country's GDP per capita is often strongly correlated with various indicators that assess the social, economic, and environmental welfare of the nation and its population. In this data, we observe that countries like Norway, the United States, and Australia have a high per capita GDP.

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) represents the total market value of all final goods and services produced within a country during a specific year. Countries are categorized based on nominal GDP estimates provided by financial and statistical institutions, which are calculated using market or official government exchange rates.

It's important to keep in mind that GDP does not account for differences in exchange rates and the cost of living among countries, and therefore, it may not accurately reflect the living standards of a country's residents.

Here we can see that the GDP of the United States and China far surpasses all other countries. Among the top five GDP countries in the world, Germany, Japan, and India have similar GDP figures, but due to the vast differences in their populations, the standard of living for their residents varies significantly.

Number of unicorn companies by countries

A unicorn company is a term used in the business and investment world to describe a privately held startup company that has reached a valuation of at least $1 billion. The term was coined to represent the rarity of such companies, similar to how mythical unicorns are rare creatures.

Unicorn companies, those valued at over $1 billion, are predominantly found in the United States due to its combination of a thriving venture capital landscape, world-renowned universities fostering innovation, a diverse talent pool, a vast domestic market, strong legal protections, a supportive entrepreneurial ecosystem, network effects created by existing tech giants, access to customers and partners, cultural encouragement of risk-taking, and a historical legacy of global tech leadership. These factors collectively make the U.S. an ideal environment for startups to flourish and achieve unicorn status. Here I'm counting all countries with fewer than 10 unicorns as others.

Life expectancy of the World's Top 5 Countries

In the above chart, we can observe the per capita life expectancy of the world's top five GDP countries and the global average. Notably, China's per capita life expectancy has seen a significant and consistent increase over the past few decades, closely tied to its rapid GDP growth. As GDP has grown, living standards have improved, leading to an extension in life expectancy.

However, among the top five GDP countries worldwide, India's per capita life expectancy has yet to reach the global average. This is due to India's vast population, which, despite a high GDP, results in a low per capita GDP and a comparatively lower standard of living, contributing to a reduced per capita lifespan.
